Our Background

Built From the Ground Up — Six Years in the Making

Ground Down grew out of practical experience in the garden room industry. It started with installations for contacts in the trade — including work that grew alongside garden room company The Green Rooms — and before long, word spread. We were installing foundations for multiple companies before we'd set out to build a business at all.

What became clear very quickly was how consistently ground screws outperform concrete for this type of work. Faster to install, no cure time, far less mess, and none of the longer-term concerns around slab settlement or cracking. As awareness of ground screws in the industry grew, it made sense to build a dedicated business around them.

Ground Down was set up to do exactly that. We are a specialist installer — not a general building contractor, not a landscaping company. Foundations are what we do, and we have been doing them for over six years across South East England.

Product Standards

Screws Built to Perform in the Ground

The ground screws we supply and install are manufactured to achieve substantial load-bearing capacities for residential and commercial foundations. They are not a lightweight product.

Manufactured to EN1090-2 Galvanized to EN ISO 1461

EN1090-2 covers the execution of steel structural components — the manufacturing standard that ensures structural integrity. EN ISO 1461 is the standard for hot-dip galvanized coatings, which protects the steel from corrosion in ground conditions over the long term.

Fully insured for commercial and domestic work. Ground Down carries full trade insurance for ground work operations. Certificates are available on request.

Common Questions

FAQ

Are ground screws guaranteed?

We do not offer a formal written guarantee, and we would encourage caution toward any installer who makes blanket guarantee claims without qualification. What we do offer is: screws manufactured to EN1090-2 and galvanized to EN ISO 1461, installation using the correct specialist machinery, a proper site assessment before and during the job, and a sign-off with the customer at the end. We install to specification, we over-specify where sensible, and we stand behind our work.

What happens if you hit something underground?

Before any installation, we use radar, cable and utility scanning equipment to check for underground services. If we encounter an obstruction — buried concrete, utility services, large roots, or ground conditions that prevent a screw being driven — we will not charge for that position. We will advise on the best alternative for that location. We also recommend that customers notify their utility provider and mark any known services before our visit.

Are ground screws suitable for my project?

Ground screws are suitable for the majority of garden rooms, cabins, decking, timber structures and similar outdoor buildings on standard residential ground. They perform reliably across clay, loam, sandy soil and most standard South East England ground types. We can advise on the right screw length and spacing for your project. For very unusual ground conditions, unusually heavy structures, or sites with significant access limitations, we will tell you upfront if a different foundation approach is more appropriate.

Do you check for underground cables or services?

Yes. We carry and use radar, cable and utility scanning equipment before every installation. This does not replace your responsibility to check with your utility provider and mark any known services before we arrive — we ask all customers to do this. If we identify a potential conflict on site, we will stop and discuss the best approach before proceeding.

Are ground screws better than concrete?

For most residential garden room and outdoor structure applications, yes. Ground screws require no excavation and produce no spoil, involve no concrete or cure time, and allow you to start building on the same day. There are no concerns about slab settlement or cracking over time. The installation is faster, cleaner and causes less disruption to the garden than a poured concrete base. For the majority of projects we work on, ground screws are the practical, cleaner and faster choice. Concrete may still be appropriate for certain specialist structures or unusual ground conditions, and we will always give you an honest assessment before you book.

Do you work on domestic projects or just trade?

Both. Most of our regular work is for garden room companies and trade customers, but domestic installations are welcome. If you are a homeowner having a garden room, cabin or outdoor structure installed and you want ground screws professionally installed, we can help.
